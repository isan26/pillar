Story 1: The Lantern Keeper of Brindle Cove
What unusual object did Elias Thorn carry with him, and what special purpose did it eventually reveal?
Who was Mira, and what problem involving the Tide Road did she ask Elias to help solve?
Why did the Tide Road begin to weaken, and what were the "waymarks" used for?
What happened to the lantern during the restoration of the Tide Road, and how did Elias prepare for the future afterward?

Story 2: The Clockmaker of Alder Square
What message did Cedric Hollow leave attached to the numberless clock, and why was it important?
What strange phenomena began occurring in Alder Square when the city's relationship with time became unbalanced?
What did the brass compass reveal to Juniper, and what lesson did she learn from the people she met?
According to Cedric's explanation, what was the true cause of the city's temporal problems?

Story 3: The Forest Beneath the Mountain
Why did the Verdant Kin hide the forest beneath Mount Ardent, and how did they keep it concealed?
What solution did Liora propose when outsiders discovered the mountain, and how did the earthquake influence the final outcome?
Bonus Analytical Questions

These require deeper understanding rather than simple recall:

What common theme connects Elias, Juniper, and Liora as protagonists?
How does each story explore the idea that preserving something valuable requires cooperation?
Which story's conflict is caused primarily by human behavior, and which are caused by external threats?
In what ways do the endings suggest that knowledge should be passed to future generations?
If you had to summarize the moral of each story in one sentence, what would it be?

Questions 11–15 are especially useful because someone who only skimmed the stories will struggle to answer them convincingly.
